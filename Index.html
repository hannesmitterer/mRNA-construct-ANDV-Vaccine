## 1. Ensilicated mRNA Vaccine (Phase‑1) **Active components** - **mRNA construct** encoding the ANDV Gn/Gc glycoproteins and nucleocapsid (NP) protein → elicits neutralising antibodies and CD8⁺ T‑cell responses. - **Ensilication® silica matrix** – patented SiO₂ “cage” that surrounds the mRNA‑protein complex, preserving integrity at ambient temperature (no cold‑chain required). **Manufacturing steps** 1. **In‑vitro transcription** of capped, poly‑A‑tailed mRNA. 2. **Complexation** with lipid nanoparticle (LNP) carrier (standard for mRNA vaccines). 3. **Silica encapsulation** using Professor Sartbaeva’s sol‑gel protocol (controlled hydrolysis of tetra‑ethyl‑orthosilicate, pH ≈ 7, 30 min). 4. **Sterile filtration** (0.22 µm) and fill‑finish into pre‑filled syringes (0.5 mL per dose). **Administration** - Sub‑cutaneous or intramuscular injection, 0.5 mL per dose. - Two‑dose schedule, 21 days apart (Phase‑1 evaluating safety, tolerability, and immunogenicity). **Current status (May 2026)** – **Phase 1** ongoing at University of Bath; primary endpoints met for safety, robust IgG and IFN‑γ responses detected up to day 42. --- ## 2. NV‑387 Re‑Infection Inhibitor **Mechanism of action** - **Nanovircide polymer** mimics the host cell‑surface glycan pattern, binding ANDV virions as they attempt budding. - Forms non‑productive virus‑polymer complexes, preventing extracellular spread → “cell‑intrinsic quarantine”. **Composition** - Core polymer: **poly(ethylene glycol)‑b‑poly(lactic acid)** backbone functionalised with **sialic‑acid‑mimetic** side chains (≈ 5 % mol). - Size distribution: 30–50 nm spherical nanoparticles, zeta potential ≈ ‑10 mV. **Clinical use** - **IV infusion** 0.5 mg kg⁻¹ over 30 min, repeated every 12 h while the patient remains in HPS‑grade III/IV. - **Monitoring** via Sentimento‑Pulse: continuous SpO₂, lung acoustic resonance, and cardiac output to detect early reversal of pulmonary edema. **Regulatory stage** – **Compassionate‑use protocol** granted in EU and US for severe HPS;
